Pro Tips & Recommendations
📸
Progress Photos Every 2 Weeks
Same lighting, same time (AM fasted), same poses. Weeks 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11. The mirror lies — photos don't. This is how you'll see the recomp happening.
⚖️
Weigh Daily, Average Weekly
Daily weight fluctuates. Weigh each morning post-bathroom, but only compare weekly averages. During recomp the scale may barely move while you're visually transforming.
🧪
Bloodwork at Week 6
Get a comprehensive panel mid-protocol — metabolic panel, lipids, fasting glucose, A1C, IGF-1. This is your mid-point check before the 4 mg ramp.
🤢
Managing GI Sides at Dose Bumps
When you jump to 2 mg and 4 mg, expect 3–7 days of nausea or fullness. Eat smaller, more frequent meals. Ginger tea or ginger chews help. Don't skip meals — shrink them.
🥤
Protein Insurance Policy
At 4 mg Reta, appetite will be crushed. Keep whey isolate or clear protein on hand always. If you can't eat solid, get 40–50g from a shake. Never let protein drop below 200g.
🧂
Electrolytes Are Non-Negotiable
Reta can cause dehydration. Sodium, potassium, magnesium daily. LMNT, Liquid IV, or salt your water. Keeps pumps full, performance high, prevents cramping.
📋
Key Milestone Check-ins
Week 4: Full stack running 2 weeks — sleep and recovery should be noticeably better. Reassess CJC/Ipa timing if not.

Week 6: Bloodwork + progress photos. Visual recomp should be undeniable. Decision point before 4 mg ramp.

Week 8: Should be ~9–10% BF. If behind, tighten nutrition. If on track, stay the course.

Week 12: Final assessment. Photos, measurements, plan next phase.
🛌
Protect Your Sleep
CJC/Ipa's biggest benefit is the GH pulse during deep sleep. Take your PM shot 15–20 min before bed. Room cold and dark. No screens 30 min prior. This is where the magic happens.
